# HOW TO GET YOUR PORTFOLIO LIVE
## A step-by-step guide for Austin Nwaigwe
### Written for someone who has never done this before. Every step counts.

---

## WHAT YOU'LL NEED
- A computer (laptop or desktop)
- About 20 minutes
- Two free accounts you'll create below

---

## PART 1 — CREATE YOUR TWO FREE ACCOUNTS

### Step 1: Create a GitHub account
GitHub is where your website files will be stored.
Think of it like Google Drive, but specifically for websites.

1. Go to: https://github.com
2. Click "Sign up"
3. Enter your email, create a password, choose a username
   (Suggestion: austinnwaigwe or austinndesign)
4. Complete the verification and confirm your email
5. You're in. Keep this tab open.

### Step 2: Create a Netlify account
Netlify is what puts your website on the internet.

1. Go to: https://netlify.com
2. Click "Sign up"
3. Choose "Sign up with GitHub" — this connects both accounts automatically
4. Authorize Netlify when it asks
5. You're in. Keep this tab open too.

---

## PART 2 — PUT YOUR FILES ON GITHUB

### Step 3: Create a new repository on GitHub
A "repository" (or "repo") is just a folder for your website.

1. On GitHub, click the green "New" button (top left)
2. Repository name: type  austin-portfolio
3. Make sure "Public" is selected
4. Check the box that says "Add a README file"
5. Click the green "Create repository" button
6. You'll see a page with your new empty repo

### Step 4: Upload your portfolio files
1. On your repo page, click "Add file" → "Upload files"
2. Open the austin-portfolio folder you downloaded from Claude
3. Select ALL the files and folders inside it (Ctrl+A or Cmd+A)
4. Drag them into the GitHub upload area
5. Scroll down, click "Commit changes"
6. Wait for the upload to finish (30–60 seconds)
7. You should now see all your files listed in the repo ✓

---

## PART 3 — CONNECT GITHUB TO NETLIFY

### Step 5: Deploy on Netlify
1. Go to your Netlify dashboard: https://app.netlify.com
2. Click "Add new site" → "Import an existing project"
3. Click "GitHub"
4. Find and click on "austin-portfolio" in the list
5. Leave all the settings as they are
6. Click "Deploy site"
7. Netlify will build your site — takes about 1 minute
8. You'll get a live URL like: magical-design-123456.netlify.app

### Step 6: Give yourself a better URL (optional but recommended)
1. On your site dashboard, click "Site settings"
2. Click "Change site name"
3. Type: austinnwaigwe  (or whatever you prefer)
4. Your new URL: austinnwaigwe.netlify.app ✓

---

## PART 4 — SET UP YOUR ADMIN PANEL (to upload future work)

### Step 7: Enable Netlify Identity
This is what lets you log into your admin panel securely.

1. On your Netlify site dashboard, click "Integrations" in the top menu
2. Search for "Identity" and click it
3. Click "Enable Identity"
4. Scroll down to "Registration" → change it to "Invite only"
   (This means only you can log in)
5. Scroll down to "Git Gateway" → click "Enable Git Gateway"

### Step 8: Invite yourself
1. Still in Identity settings, click "Invite users"
2. Enter YOUR email address
3. Check your email — you'll get an invite
4. Click the link in the email and set a password
5. That password is what you'll use to log into /admin

### Step 9: Log into your admin panel
1. Go to: https://austinnwaigwe.netlify.app/admin
   (replace "austinnwaigwe" with your actual site name)
2. Log in with your email and password
3. You'll see a panel that says "Projects" on the left
4. Click "New Project" to add your first piece of work
5. Fill in the title, category, upload your image, add a description
6. Click "Publish" — your site updates automatically ✓

## ADDING YOUR REAL WORK IMAGES

Right now the site shows your project titles as a list.
To add actual images:

1. Log into /admin
2. Click "Projects"
3. Click on a project (e.g. "4TS Technologies")
4. Upload your project image under "Cover Image"
5. Add more images under "Gallery Images" if you want
6. Click "Publish"

The site will update within 1–2 minutes.

## CONNECTING A CUSTOM DOMAIN (e.g. austinnwaigwe.com)

When you're ready to get your own domain name:
1. Buy a domain from Namecheap.com or Google Domains (usually $10–$15/year)
2. In Netlify: Site settings → Domain management → Add custom domain
3. Follow the instructions — Netlify walks you through it step by step
4. Your site will be live at your own domain within 24 hours
